= Intent Gatekeeper
:description: How to control which Android apps are allowed to open links in WebLibre.

When another app on your device tries to open a link in the browser, WebLibre can ask whether to allow or block it.
This privacy feature is called the *Intent Gatekeeper*.

It helps when:

* a notification or background app keeps redirecting you into the browser
* an app opens unexpected links that you did not ask for
* you want to limit which apps can hand traffic to your browser

== How It Works

When the Intent Gatekeeper is on, WebLibre checks the *source app* every time another Android app tries to open a URL.

For each source app, WebLibre can:

* prompt you the first time and remember the decision
* allow the app to open links
* block the app from opening links

You stay in control of the policy per app, not per link.

== Open the Settings

. Go to *Settings > Privacy & Security*
. Open the *Intent Gatekeeper* screen

From there you can:

* enable or disable the gatekeeper
* review the list of source apps and their saved decisions
* change a saved decision back to *Ask* or flip it between *Allow* and *Block*

== Notifications and Quick Replies

When the gatekeeper blocks a link, WebLibre can post a notification with action buttons so you can respond without opening the browser.

The available actions are:

* *Always allow* -- whitelist the source app permanently
* *Allow once* -- open this single link without saving the decision

*Always allow* appears first in the notification so you do not accidentally tap *Allow once* when you meant to whitelist the app for good.

You can also configure how the gatekeeper communicates with you in *Gatekeeper Notification Options* on the same settings screen.

== Changing or Resetting Decisions

To change what an app is allowed to do:

. Open *Settings > Privacy & Security > Intent Gatekeeper*
. Find the source app in the list
. Pick a new decision

If you want every app to ask again the next time it tries to open a link, reset its decision to *Ask*.

== Things to Expect

* The gatekeeper only intercepts links that other apps try to open in WebLibre. It does not affect links you tap inside WebLibre.
* If you set WebLibre as your default browser, the gatekeeper is what ultimately decides whether a request from another app reaches a tab.
* Source apps that you have allowed once still have to follow your other settings, such as xref:custom-tabs-and-external-links.adoc[External Link Handling].
